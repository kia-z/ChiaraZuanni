Chiara Zuanni

I am an associate professor in digital humanities, with a focus on museology, based at the University of Graz.

Research Interests

Digital representations of museum histories

I am interested in researching and developing ways for visualising museum histories with digital media, considering both collection and archival data, as well as 3D and immersive representations of the museum spaces.

Born digital heritage

Late 20th and 21st century heritage is often born digital: I am interested in how museums are acquiring, preserving, interpreting, and displaying this material and in the potential synergies between the web archiving community and contemporary collecting projects.

Public Perceptions of Archaeology

In my PhD, I researched the influence of public perceptions of archaeology on museums - and I am still interested in observing uses of the past and cultural heritage, especially in relation to museums and the digital sphere.

Museum data

I also am interested in digitisation, data standardisation and data-driven research in museum collections. On the one hand, I am interested in the digital representation of museum objects (e.g. modelling object biographies); on the other hand, I am interested in researching "collections as data", especially in relation to collection histories and histories of curation.

Digital Audience Research

I use data-driven methods and digital ethnographies to research digital museum audiences. I am interested in understanding how audience experiences are affected by digital mediation and communication and I am interested in audience benchmarks and evaluation in digital projects.

Digital skills in the heritage sector

I have worked on few projects aiming to develop training resources for the museum and heritage sector, and I am interested in further contributing to increase digital skills and expertise in museums. After my work on the so-called "digital turn" during COVID-19, I am also interested in the development of digital strategies in the sector.
